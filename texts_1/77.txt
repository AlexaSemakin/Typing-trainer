She knew Gerda directly, and Gerda remembered her: it was a joyful meeting. "You are a fine fellow to go gadding about in this way," said she to little Kay, "I should like to know whether you deserve that any one should go to the end of the world to find you. "But Gerda patted her cheeks, and asked after the prince and princess. "They are gone to foreign countries," said the robber-girl. "And the crow?" asked Gerda. "Oh, the crow is dead," she replied; "his tame sweetheart is now a widow, and wears a bit of black worsted round her leg. She mourns very pitifully, but it is all stuff.
